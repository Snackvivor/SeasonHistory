Ahem. The auction will work as follows:
19:32
Up for auction are six trays containing meals. Some will be plated and easy to see, while others will be on covered serving platters. Mysteries will ONLY be revealed to auction winners, though the winners may share the results if they wish.
19:32
Oh also the “meals” are not meals at all. They are items that may help or hurt your game.
3
19:33
You will each have 500 tokens with which to bid on trays. You must bid in increments of 10 tokens. At the end of the auction, you will surrender your tokens to the production team. There is no benefit to keeping tokens after the auction.
19:33
To bid, you will go to the channel for the tray you want to bid on. You will then make your bid. Just type the amount you want to bid and nothing else. Subsequent bidders will do the same.
19:34
Notes about bidding: - Your first counter does not need to be 10 tokens higher. You can counter a bid with a match. If one player bids 10 tokens, you and any other potential bidders may “match.” It will be the responsibility of the original bidder to raise if desired. However, you do not need to match. If you’re in a hurry, you can go higher at your leisure. - Remember, you must only go in increments of 10 tokens. So if you want to go all in, you have to work your way up to it.
19:34
- You may not bid more than you have tied up in any other item. For example, if you have bid 50 of your tokens on Tray #2, you may not exceed total bid(s) of 450 tokens on other items unless you have been outbid. Another example, if your bid of 50 tokens on tray #2 is outbid with 60 tokens and you do not counter, your original 50 tokens are now ‘free’ to use in your next bid.
19:35
You may not take back a bid after you have submitted it. So be careful when bidding. Keep in mind that production can see all of the edits made in the channels and will verify the honor of the auction. Anyone found to be breaking the rules purposefully will be disqualified. (edited)
2
19:35
19:35
But what happens if multiple people having matching bids at the end?
19:36
If several players have closing bids of equal amounts on any items, the winner will be chosen by random draw. For example, if two players bid all 500 tokens on a single item, the winner will be chosen randomly. If at the end of the auction two players have both bid 50 tokens on a single item, the same process will apply.
19:37
The auction will run from a designated start time (when I open the bidding channels with an explicit message) until 7pm EST tomorrow. You may place your bids at any time, and also bid more than the player before you at your leisure. A note about this auction: you MAY discuss it with your tribemates, but only your tribemates in the tribe chat. You cannot discuss it in 1-on-1s or alliances
19:37
19:37
There will also be a sniping guard in place. If any player bids in the last 5 minutes of the challenge and “snipes” a bid from another player, (6:55pm EST -- 7pm EST) that player will have 5 minutes AFTER the challenge end time ( until 7:05 pm EST) to place a counter bid if they desire/are able. It will be the responsibility of the players to pay attention to the bids, however, production will honor edits made in this sniping grace period for the relevant parties.
19:37
The highest bidders at the end of the auction period will be awarded their tray(s).
19:37
If you have any questions about the auction, please feel free to ping production in your submissions.
