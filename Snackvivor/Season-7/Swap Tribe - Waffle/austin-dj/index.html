Mala's early life was pretty standard. I'm not sure what exactly that entails for gnome culture, but he didn't have big dreams of adventuring or anything of that sort. Until he met Lucillia, another gnome who had moved to the area recently. They fell in love rapidly, and Lucillia's fascination with exploration led him out of his small town and further and further into the unknown. An early expedition to an abandoned laboratory captured his heart almost as much as Lucillia did and led him to pursue artificery, scooping up some supplies from the lab as his first tools. They married after fourteen years, when Mala was in his 90s, having been engaged for six years but waiting for the right expedition to come up for them to best reap the benefits of Ceremony. Following a few more years, their life as they knew it came to a halt when Lucillia was found to be pregnant, and they set out on a whole new adventure -- parenting. Their daughter Vivien was the light of their life, and they settled down to ensure she grew up with a stable home. They built a house close enough to civilisation to be in the world, but far enough that they could live surrounded by the nature that enchanted Lucillia so. While their expeditions had eventually turned profitable enough to sustain their life through raising a family, Mala couldn't shake the drive to keep being productive, leading him to finally make his own professional laboratory underneath their house where he could experiment with new potions and elixirs, partially to sell to adventurers but mostly to chase a new high of creating something nobody else could.
11:13 PM
And that's where everything went wrong, a few weeks before Vivien's sixth birthday. It was a normal day tinkering in the lab, for the most part. He couldn't remember what happened -- a faulty ingredient, a misread label, a scam from a shady foreign supplier -- but next thing he knew, glass was flying everywhere, followed by embers, and suddenly the entire room erupted in flames. Then the house. The blaze beat him upstairs, and by the time he made it to Vivien's room... they were sleeping, tiny hand in slightly less tiny hand, unmoving. Too late. And while everything in him told him to lie down, accept the inferno and join them wherever they ended up, his feet pushed him out the window and further, eventually passing out across a nearby river. When he woke up, he returned, and through more tears than he'd ever known, laid Lucillia and Vivien to rest, burying Lucillia with her favourite bow, and Vivien with her favourite toy. Pocketing one rusted vial he recognised from his original set as a reminder of the world that had bewitched and now ruined him, he smashed the remaining few that were still intact, and set foot away. His trip into town led to the docks, then to the first boat he saw. "Take me anywhere."
11:14 PM
Anywhere turned out to be a place called Galderia, where the next sixty years passed in a blur of working anywhere that would take a bitter, deeply despondent man who had nothing to do beyond living to work and occasionally drinking from a seemingly bottomless glass. His skills waned as he refused to even think about brewing a potion, and his already small stature turned hollow. Until one day, where a new sign in the Sparkling Lantern appeared, seeking new members for an Adventurer's Guild (correct this if I screwed up the details it's been like two years). For a second, Lucillia's face appeared in the sign, and occupied his mind the entire way home. He looked at his room, imagining what she would think. Then stopping, because that hurt. His last savings went to a new Alchemist's Kit, slowly relearning how to create -- he'd need it very soon. A job needs tools, after all.
