1st - DJ 9/20/2024 8:07 PM
Hey Jolly! Hope school's been treating ya well. 1. Unequivocally, overcooking the kitchen at Final 6. Everything else I can explain at least conceptually what the benefits for my game were, but Final 6 was entirely caused by getting lost in the thrill of the game to strategic detriment. My idea for the vote was a 2-2-2 tie, where:
  • Robo and Chase would vote Jah
  • Tai and Jah would vote Robo
  • Becca and myself would vote Chase.
On the revote, only Becca, Tai and I would be able to vote, and Becca/I would have majority on Chase. I knew this wasn't a great move when going through it. While Becca and I had Jah's loyalty at the Final 6 vote, if we made this happen it would likely cause him to work with Robo and Tai at F5 and take out whichever of Becca/I lost immunity. At the same time, if Tai was lying about voting for Robo, it opened up a 3-2-1 on either Becca or Jah, both of which would make my life harder going forward. In the end, the latter happened, but from the explanation I was given post-tribal, not in the way I expected. Becca also tried to get Tai targeting Chase, ending up 3-2-1ing him. This information instead gave Tai confirmation we were not putting 3 votes on Robo, giving him the opening to take the plurality shot at Becca (Tai, I've read your comments, we talked about this at F5 -- the plan to vote Becca was made well in advance but leaking the 2-2-2 guaranteed it would work). I was close to telling Jah right before tribal to get 3 votes going on Chase, but decided against it as I didn't want to upset Becca by screwing with the plan. This was undeniably a mistake, and the thing I would change if I had the chance to do it again. This would have forced a 3-3 tie where Jah claimed to be willing to go to rocks for Becca, hopefully getting through the tribal without either of them leaving, either through someone on the other side folding or winning the 2/3 rock draw. 2. Honestly, this one didn't change much the entire time -- there were very few points at which Jah was not the F2 I wanted to sit with. That being said, the points I questioned it are worth discussion. Final 11: This is when Jah and I made our F2, and I planned to honour it. Up until that point though, I had other close allies it would be cool to sit with. The main person here was Robo, who was my closest ally throughout pre-swap and we reconnected well at merge. While our games drifted apart fairly quickly, our personal connection never did. Final 10: Not much changed -- I know I idoled Jamie, but I never planned to take them to 2. During swap, they told me directly that they would need to "take [my] social ass out late merge", and I didn't think the idol would change this. I was planning on letting them go, or even taking them out myself, by around Final 8-7 to get ahead of this, but the circumstances of how this tribal played out led to me deciding now wasn't the right time. Still Jah! Final 9: Still Jah, which reinforced itself as tribal happened and Jah and I were left associated with each other more publicly -- I may have burnt trust with him at Final 10, but he didn't exactly have better options on the other side of the fence. Final 8: Jah! I did start getting closer with Becca around this time, and she was someone I grew to trust a lot and even see as a #1 at F7, but I would have been a fool to bring her given the choice.
8:27 PM
Final 7: Here's where things wavered, as I found out Jah was setting up to turn on me -- while going to F2 with him would still be cool, the path towards it became a lot less stable. I started to consider other options -- Foolish if I could find a way to keep them, or Chase. This was more process of elimination than anything, as I didn't feel comfortable going with Becca, and Tai and Robo's games felt significantly stronger than they were presenting them to be. After hashing things out with Jah though, the cycle ended with him back on top. Final 6: I locked back in with Jah for good. Not much more to say here. Final 5: Despite nearly going home, Jah was still my ideal Final 2, both because I wanted to be there with him and that he was my best chance of getting there at all. If he were voted out this tribal, it would have been Chase, for the same reason as at Final 7. Final 4: Honestly, I would have gone to F2 with whoever gave me fire here. If it were Robo, taking him over Chase is probably objectively the wrong move, but my heart wouldn't allow me to cut him. Final 3: Jah.
