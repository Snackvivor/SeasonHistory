Episode One: "Ways to make a bad first impression" - PBJ Sake: Spirits were high at the Sake tribe, as the energetic new castaways dominated in the first challenge. Jenna was able to do a lot of legwork for the more specific items, while Pickles and Kyran focused on art, and Zacharie and Winley on video tasks. However, everyone contributed to a variety of categories on the challenge, allowing them to keep up a quick pace. Sake were able to come together to complete more teamwork oriented tasks than any tribe, netting them the highest score in the challenge of 39 out of a possible 48 points! On the social side, Jenna and Pickles were able to connect early and quickly become each others most trusted, establishing a flow of information on their tribe that netted them both CPs. Zacharie was one of the quieter voices on the tribe and in the challenge, but their impeccable selfie game and charisma had them viewed positively by the tribe and got them a low visibility MORP rating. In contrast, after Winley had a very productive performance at the beginning of the challenge, people felt conversations stalling out in 1on1s which caused them to fall to the bottom of the trust rankings. That, combined with Winley feeling sick and being unavailable to contribute after the night of the challenge announcement got them a MORM rating. Kyran had a decent prescience in the challenge, but connected most to Zacharie who was around the least, and was immediately wary of Jenna who he perceived to be a strong threat. Overall, Kyrans lack of connection to active players caused him to drop in the trust rankings and got him a MOR for the episode. Unagi: The Unagi tribe was a storm of social activity, especially for not having a tribal to go to! Right off the bat, Etay, PBJ, and Annie are chatting it up and quickly realize that they all want to work together. They form an alliance they call APEs, and begin talking there regularly. This earns PBJ and Annie CP ratings for the episode, as they were members of the key trio on Unagi as well as did great work on the challenge. Etay gets an OTT though, for being a bit too focused on the immediate social activity of the tribe. His constant emphasis on one-on-one message counts showed that he might read too much into certain details of the game. The other tribe members, Fones and Stephen were notably less present than the APEs trio. They contributed some to the challenge and some to the social game, but it was noted they were clearly less active than the others. This gets them both UTR ratings, since they kept up with the tribe but were not major contributors to the dynamics.

Ebi: The Ebi tribe was a bit of a mixed bag in terms of player activity and sociability. Noah and Taco were fairly inactive at the start, leading Mana, Sky and Treb to come together as a trio, reinforced by their activity and performance in the challenge. While they ultimately were not able to pick up enough slack to avoid tribal, Treb created an alliance with Mana and Sky called Slay-Ebiday which would become crucial for the next few votes. Taco sensing that he may be in danger attempted to form an alliance with Treb and Sky, but it was ultimately unsuccessful. The Ebidays had to decide between Taco and Noah and although Taco was more active, the overall negative reception to his challenge performance coupled with the understanding that Noah was busy the first two days was enough to lean towards Taco. The vote would be a simple 4-1 with Taco being eliminated and Noah receiving the stray vote. Treb gets a CPP for his overall positive perception from the tribe, his challenge performance, strategic musings and for bringing together the Slay-Ebiday group. Mana and Sky both get P tones for their upbeat attitudes and reception from their tribemates. Taco gets an N tone for his slight attitude during the challenge which rubbed his tribemates the wrong way. Noah gets a UTR for being basically inactive for the first two days outside of a couple short messages. Hokkigai: The Hokkigai tribe got off to a much slower start out of the gate, seeing significantly less activity in comparison to the other tribes. Dylan became unable to participate in the game due to irl commitments, and was substituted for Aura. The more hands-off approach of Hokkigai was felt not only in the minimal communication among the tribe, but in the scavenger hunt bingo challenge as well. The first night of the challenge saw Dicy be the only member to submit any items, and not a single bingo was scored until the next day. After a night of relaxing, Slaytan went into overdrive and ended up contributing the most items of any castaway. Rob and Aura were able to add a few items to the tally as well. Sadly, this effort was not enough to overcome the early deficit, and Hokkigai faced the first tribal council of the season. On a more positive note, there was a clear consensus among the tribe with regard to the direction of the vote. Leroy had been completely inactive since the marooning, failing to contribute to the challenge or discuss the impending vote, resulting in his unanimous elimination. Due to the straightforward nature of this cycle, no official alliances were formed, although the social center of the tribe appears to be the pair of Dicy and Slaytan. Dicy nets the only CP rating of the tribe for his early positioning as well as the strong game aptitude displayed in his confessionals. MORs and UTRs abound for the remaining members of the tribe, with the exception of Leroy, whose INV status led to his permanent disappearance from the game in the form of a torch snuff.
