Pie, the Apple-Filled 2/24/2024 10:03 PM
20 This is my review of the Snackaversary Choose Your Own Adventure Game. To preface, I will only be discussing the route that I took to Pastadise Island, so not every aspect of the game will be discussed. With that said, let's get into it! The first thing I noticed was that the game was taking heavy inspiration from visual novels, a genre of game I have next to no familiarity with. Despite this, I was instantly charmed by the art of the game. The backgrounds were all well-made, and each represented the intended location of each conversation well. More important, however, are the character portraits, and these did not disappoint either. Each character I encountered had a distinct feel to them, which kept me interested and made me excited to move on to the next scene. Each also represented the food item they were based on well, which was neat. Particular standouts from my run were Kaptain Klondike, Martini, and Key Lime. So as I've laid out, the visual part of this visual novel was executed quite well, so now we must look at the novel part. The dialogue in the game was fairly basic, but given that so much had to be written, that isn't much of a criticism. The main conflict of the story is trying to find a way to Pastadise Island. In most cases, each character you meet will attempt to assist you in your goal, and in the route I took only one character, Drumstick, was unhelpful. So the vibes of the game are pretty positive overall. And that really summarizes my overall thoughts on the game. It's a small fun little jaunt through Snackvivor locals, brimming with positive vibes and great art. This game gets a thumbs-up from me!

Pie, the Apple-Filled 2/24/2024 10:34 PM
55 This is a Dolly Neely appreciation post. Dolly lasted two episodes on Survivor's ninth season, Vanuatu. As the tribes that season were divided by gender, she was placed on the all-female Yasur tribe. On Yasur, there were two major alliances of four: the older women (Twila, Leann, Ami, Scout) who wanted everyone to work hard around camp, and the younger women (Mia, Eliza, Lisa, Julie) who thought the older women were being too demanding. Dolly was in the middle, conflicted. She was younger, sure, but she knew the value of hard work. After all, she was a sheep farmer from Pennsylvania, hard work was part of her day-to-day life. Because of this, she felt connected to people on both sides of the tribe. When Yasur lost immunity in episode 2, Dolly would be forced to make a decision. She did NOT want to though. She spent most of the day waffling between both sides, not wanting to hurt anyone's feelings. A sweet sentiment to be sure, but not one that works on Survivor. In the end, she was able to convince the younger women to vote for Leann instead of Twila, and in return she would side with them at them vote. However, her wishy-washyness throughout the day caught her the ire of the ever-paranoid Eliza Orlins. Eliza decided she did want to be in an alliance with Dolly and decided to flip, voting with the older women for Dolly. And so after six days in Vanuatu, Dolly was eliminated in a 5-4 vote, and inadvertently helped create the alliance that would run much of the season from that moment on. So, for someone not many of us think about, she had an impressively large footprint on the history of Survivor.
