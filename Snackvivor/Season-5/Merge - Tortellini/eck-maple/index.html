Okay combat advice; 1. Ask your players to think ahead of time for their turn. 2. Don't be afraid to modify numbers of creature stars on the fly but keep it consistent. 3. Your players will choose weird options lean IN and AGAINST them. Ex. If a player has Fire resistance give them enemies that deal fire damage, if they have weird like shove feats give them a cliff and a precarious enemy. Don't be the GM that doesn't use poison against dwarves because 'They are resistant to it why would I throw it at them.' 4. Make your enemies dumb or smart. It's dumb for an enemy to attack the front line and ignore the damage dealers in the back, that's great. It's smart for enemies to consider someone down a non - threat and not double tap them. 5. Rules are still a thing! Try not to make too many exceptions or special rulings, if a player dies that every turn it's rough on you and everyone else following the rules correctly. 6. They are boobies don't be afraid to tell them enemy hp or AC. Make it a point to tell players enemies have a low ability score to see if they can take advantage of it.
