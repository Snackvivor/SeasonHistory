Pyers 2/25/2024 10:59 PM
So if I'm looking at my past games and thinking about the lessons I've learned, I think there's a couple obvious things: From my first ever ORG, I learned that winning challenges is all that matters and also having a super tight secret alliance that no one knows about is sick even if it's so secret you never manage to vote together. I won. In my second (much more intensive ORG), I fucking smashed the pre-merge tribe phase and damn near never lost a challenge. The one I lost, I did that nice combination of pulling the strings and letting other people drive to get exactly what I really wanted out of the tribal vote. Then that tribe split up and I came to the merge completely unaware of the fact that while I'd been chilling on the beach challenge beast-ing for 2 weeks, they were all actually playing survivor. What I've learned from this is that you gotta play all the time and keep playing even when you're winning, and cultivate a really good relationship with your tribemates so that when the time comes, they tell you the important things first, and that initial tribal line alliances don't mean fuck all - but the alliances made on tribes right before the merge do...sort of. In my third ORG, I actually managed to lose some pre-merge challenges and be involved in voting some people out. They were almost all pretty easy boots that were more on inactivity (actually 2 of the 3 were withdrawals) but we at least went through the process of losing a tribe member. On my pre-merge tribe, I cultivated strong relationships with most of my tribemates and at least talked in theory about what we might do at the merge, which was super important. However, I did get "big play-itis" a little bit early in the merge because I was worried that the game was going somewhere boring and got cooking too early. But man oh man, was I cooking - I just didn't cook hard enough. I learned that once you get going and have shots to shoot, hit them, and make waves, especially when you see something dangerous developing that could hurt you. I was overconfident in using a key advantage to "restore the balance" of a game to allow myself to keep playing the middle, when I should have shot my shot at one of the people most likely to send me packing, which they kindly did at the exact next opportunity. So to summarize these may or not have really been covered above but they're the real answer to moliwog's question, the rest is more context: Win challenges good, fun, awesome, but you have to lose some to ultimately win, no one's gonna let you go too far if you Joe Anglim the pregame unless you JT the merge game. I'm good but I'm not that good. Cultivate the premerge alliances and test them by giving and taking at the merge. Don't rely on allegiances that happened before the merge unless they've been backed up by a recent vote. If something seems fishy, press on it and find the weak point instead of resigning yourself to losing. Don't be afraid to play a dominant game or pagong if it helps you fly under the radar, once you make your big move you have to put your foot on the gas pedal and take out anyone who recognizes it. Talk to everyone every round even if you know for a fact they have every intention to send you packing or if you know for a fact that they're going home.
