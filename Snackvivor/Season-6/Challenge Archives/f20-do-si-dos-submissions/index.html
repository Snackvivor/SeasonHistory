Sometimes games just take too long for the introduction and they leave me waiting for too long (I lost interest FAST!). This is particularly helpful for someone like that enjoys cutting “straight to the chase” in games. Good examples of games that do this kind of storytelling well are Detroit Become Human or Life is strange – the former being a better comparison to this, since it usually involves 2 choices the player can take. Rocket is one of the characters that instantly made an impact, looking badass. Might I just add that I love how enthusiastic us as the narrator is! Thennnnn comes my favorite character! Chipwich. He’s a dapper fellow that recited, what I consider, very iconic lines. Behold: “Excusez-mois?” and “What Say Thee?”. Enough said. At one point, I decided to throw caution to the wind and just go hitchhiking. There, I met someone named Ebi on the side of the road and… honestly, I was a little unsettled by the encounter. I as the player had a whole inner monologue before coming across this ‘shady guy’. When given the chose as to whether to follow the shady guy or not, I clicked ‘follow’ immediately There, I got to meet Blueberry - who, I have to say, loves to say the word “boo”. Like, I think this was her idea of a prank but it didn’t affect me at all. Sorry Blueberry… You look great though, and after talking to you I got to head to the Sushitopia Docks. It’s time to set sail on a pirate ship.

- - -

Aesthetics wise, the art style is simple but effective. One good stylistic choice is that the character turns black and white when you are speaking during dialogue, making it easier to keep track of who says what. Story-wise, I like that we can discover the area and really branch out with the choices. It is very clear that the creators spent a lot of time building the lore, creating the characters and allowing us to branch out. I think what would have been a nice addition, even though it would be a lot of extra work, is to add some nice sound effects and background music. It would make the whole thing a lot more immersive and really hook me more. Even just some dialogue noises (the classic ‘beeping’ transcript sound you get in VN games) would have done the trick. I want to feel like I’m really part of the world. All in all, I think this was a great Choose Your Adventure game! I didn’t have time to play through the whole thing, but it’s very extensive and you can kind of lean into whatever approach you like. Can’t wait to see what else Snackvivor produces, and I highly recommend playing The Search For Pastadise yourself!
