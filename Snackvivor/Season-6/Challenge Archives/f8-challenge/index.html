# F8: MANCOOKIELA !!!
8:29 PM
This is essentially just Mancala, but in bot form! Fish made @Girl Scout Gang War (this was a failed season name) to help facilitate. If you’ve never played Mancala before, it’s pretty simple. Mancala is a two-player board game that involves moving stones around pits. The board has two rows of six pits each, with a larger store at each end – one for each player. Each pit starts with 4 stones (or cookies, for our game) in it. The goal is to collect the most cookies in your store by the end of the game!
8:29 PM
The board will look like this:
8:30 PM
8:30 PM
Player 1 will be purple, and Player 2 will be yellow. The colorful blobs are your pits, and the tents are the stores.
8:30 PM
On your turn, you will do !play # to pick up all of the cookies from one of your pits. The bot will drop one cookie into each subsequent pit, moving counterclockwise along the board. If you reach your store, it will place a cookie in there and then continue until there are no more left. If you have enough cookies to make it all the way around to your opponent's store, you will NOT place a cookie in their store. The bot will send an image of the board with the updated pieces, and will prompt the next person for their turn.
8:31 PM

Special Cases

  • Extra Turn: If your last cookie ends up in your OWN store, you immediately get another turn. The bot will tell you this.
  • Capturing: If your last cookie is placed in an empty pit on YOUR side of the board, and there are cookies in the pit directly opposite of that one (indicated by the shapes in the bottom left corner of the pits for clarity) – you capture both your cookies and all of the cookies in your opponent’s pit opposite of yours. All of these cookies go directly into your store.
 (edited)
8:31 PM
There will be times in the game when you have no cookies on your side, but your opponent does. The bot will still make it “your turn” – when this happens, use the command !no-moves to keep the game moving. You may NOT use this command if you have cookies on your side that you can move. (edited)
8:32 PM
The game ends when BOTH sides have been completely emptied. The counters in the tents will be keeping track along the way – the person with the most cookies in their tent at the end of the game wins! If a game ties (since that is possible with an even number of cookies), you will simply play again.
8:33 PM
