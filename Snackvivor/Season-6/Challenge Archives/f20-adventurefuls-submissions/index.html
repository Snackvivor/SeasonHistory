With it's iconic three-horned skull and quadrupedal build, the triceratops truly shows that we can't judge a book by its cover, it defies our expectation and is truly a gentle giant. But it's dominant in it's own way! It was one of the most dominant herbivores of the era, making up over 5/6s of the herbivore population for it's time. Did you know triceratops had a beak? They did! In one of the funny quirks of evolution, something that we associate with birds now was once grounded with our good friend, the triceratops. But, not only that, they served a vital purpose. Triceratops would knock over huge trees with their horns and their beak; putting that large stature to good use as they manage to destroy some of the largest and most durable bits of nature to ever exist. As for other practical uses, the triceratops was never one to back down to a fight. When push came to shove, they would use their huge size and magnificent horns to battle any predator foolish enough to stand in its way. But a triceratops never uses these powers for evil, but rather for self-defense. Arguably the largest contribution the triceratops has made was after its own death. As a fossil, the triceratops lived for a very specific amount of time which makes it incredibly useful in terms of locating what time period other animals lived. As a result, we have a much more accurate assessment of how and where other prehistoric animals lived, and it's partially thanks to the triceratops! It's also a key in understanding evolution. As I alluded to earlier, triceratops have beaks, which is an odd quality for a quadrupedal herbivore to have, but the reality is that, much like a lot of other dinosaurs, those genetics came to become modern birds. In fact, birds are the most closely related animal on the planet to the dinosaurs we hold in such high regard. As an additional fun fact, Triceratops are the official state dinosaur for the state of Wyoming! I'm not entirely sure what the entails or what it means, especially given Wyoming is a lie created by the government for tax purposes, but it means something I'm sure of it! There is a common misconception because a scientist mistakenly painted his idea of a tyrannosaurus rex and a triceratops together, but in reality the two likely never met all that much. Due to location, triceratops were mostly located in northwestern North America, and tyrannosaurus rex usually didn't spend a lot of time up there. But it's helped paint these two colossal figures as what we imagine in our head when we think "dinosaurs". Today, I played the Snackvivor Anniversary Choose Your Own Adventure Game! It was a cute visual novel, that focused on the protagonist trying to get to the legendary Pastadise Island. Filled with dramatic twists and turns, as well as quirky characters who either bar your way or assist you on your journey. Beginning with the only character that you are truly required to meet, Brie! In your search for the map, Brie is surprisingly helpful! She gives you the map to Pastadise with little more than a snide remark, and points you in the direction you need to go. Brie opts to tell you that you will never get there, which is a weird statement. In fact, almost everyone in the entire experience is not only quick to help you, but will assist you on your way to Pastadise. I think this is intentional, and a reflection that sometimes the hardest part of a journey is getting over the mental struggle. To take the dive into something you may not be fully prepared for is a very scary thing, and Brie exemplifies that within the context of the story. While we the player are able to step away, and recover at any time; the us that is in the game is told that they will never make it. But this is contradicted. Sometimes we are underestimated, and we can defy expectations, whether it is our own or the expectations of others. After that, I met with Kaptain Klondike (the K is intentional, I promise) who was the main way of getting to Pastadise. Klondike is a bit rough around the edges, calling you short, and openly mocking you; so a bit more confrontational than Brie, but is still open to helping you. She takes you on a trip to Pastadise if you can catch her boat on time... which I did not.
